Creative and Visual AI

Eye for the
Extraordinary

I am not a trained designer, and that is exactly the point.

My background is in writing, economics, and making complex things clear. But the more I work at the intersection of language and visual communication, the more I feel the pull of how things look, not just how they read.

AI generative tools are where those two instincts meet. A prompt is just writing with visual intent, and as someone who has spent years choosing the right word, I find that prompting comes naturally. It is the same muscle, a different medium.

This section is me learning in public. These are concept experiments, strategy exercises, and visual thinking — the beginning of a practice, not the end of one.

The Visual
AI Strategy

If I were directing the visual content strategy for The On‑the‑Go Collection, this is how I would approach it. Using AI generation not to replace craft, but to prototype, iterate, and produce at a pace a small brand can actually afford.

01

Foundation

Write the visual language before generating a single image

Every visual begins as a brief, written the same way I would write UX copy: clear, specific, and intentional. A well‑written brief makes every generated image more consistent across the whole campaign.

02

Generate

Build three product shoot variants per SKU

Using Midjourney or Firefly to produce lifestyle product shots: sachet in hand, sachet on surface, sachet in context of use. Ten iterations per scenario, with the best three selected per pack.

03

Channels

Instagram, TikTok, and editorial gifting

Static editorial imagery for the Instagram grid. Short‑form video concepts for TikTok centred around "what I add to everything." PR gifting kits sent to food editors and wellness journalists alongside real sample sachets.

04

Story

Lead with function, land with flavour

The magnesium and electrolyte angle is the hook for health‑conscious audiences. The flavour story — heat, sweet, herb — is the hook for food lovers. Content alternates between the two without ever contradicting either.

05

Iteration

Treat prompt variations like A/B copy tests

Each visual prompt is a hypothesis. Change one variable — light, background, model gesture — measure engagement and then refine. Visual strategy and content strategy are the same discipline once you see them that way.

06

Scale

Thirty assets from three core prompts

A single well‑crafted prompt system can generate a full month of content. Seasonal variations, platform crops, and new colour moods all derived from one visual language document — written once, extended indefinitely.

How I work, from brief to visual

Step 01 — Intent

Write the feeling before generating the frame

Every image starts as a written brief — the same discipline as UX copy. Specific, purposeful, and built around what the audience needs to feel, not just see.

Minimal studio. A woman's hand, calm and unhurried, holding a small red sachet against warm concrete. Shallow depth. Quiet heat.

Step 02 — Iterate

Refine through language, not luck

Prompt engineering is editing. Each version removes ambiguity and adds intention, adjusting mood, lighting, style weight, and negative prompts until the output earns its place.

Precision in language leads to precision in output.

Step 03 — Curate

Select with an editorial eye

Generation is not the end, selection is. Choosing which image carries the visual truth of the brief is the same instinct as choosing the right word over a good one.

The best prompt writers are editors first.
