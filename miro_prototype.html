Global nav bar Used SharePoint's built-in nav component. Active state on "Writing Services". Search bar added for discoverability.
Hero copy Clear headline + one-line value prop. Two CTAs: primary action (submit) and secondary (browse). Background: brand blue gradient.
Service cards (6) One card per content type. Icon + title + short desc + CTA link. Hover state: blue border. Grid: 3 columns.
Intake form Captures name, dept, content type (dropdown), deadline, and brief. Submit + Save Draft actions. Connects to SharePoint list.
Left navigation Persistent sidebar with grouped links. Active state highlights current page. Quick access to style guide, templates & FAQ.

Design Decisions

What I thought through
on the Miro board.

01
Sidebar for wayfinding
An internal tool needs fast navigation. A persistent left sidebar lets users jump between sections without going back to the homepage.
02
Hero clarifies purpose instantly
Employees landing here may not know what the team offers. The hero uses plain language to explain who this is for and what to do next.
03
Service cards over a text list
Cards are scannable. Each one follows the same structure — what it is, what's included, and a direct CTA — so users can self-serve quickly.
04
Form on the same page
Placing the intake form directly below the service cards removes friction. Users understand what's available and can request it in one scroll.
